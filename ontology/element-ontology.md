# Element Ontology — the 15 element types

Each element names *what a piece of learning content is*. Classify the **primary** element of a slide
first; a slide may carry secondary elements on distinct content blocks, but keep **one primary
element per slide** (mirrors the one-primary-interaction rule).

IDs use an `E` prefix and are **distinct from** the `P0`–`P8` interaction patterns.

---

## E1 — Concept
A single idea or term the learner must understand.
**Signals:** "what is…", a definition, a glossary term. **Example:** "What is an AI workflow?"

## E2 — Principle / Rule
A rule, law, policy, or guideline to follow.
**Signals:** "must/always/never", a standard, a policy clause. **Example:** "Always confirm before deleting."

## E3 — Process / Procedure
An ordered sequence of steps; a how-to or workflow.
**Signals:** numbered steps, "first… then… finally". **Example:** "How to submit a claim."

## E4 — Structure / Anatomy
The parts of a whole; components of a system, diagram, or interface.
**Signals:** a diagram with regions, "consists of", labeled parts. **Example:** "Parts of the dashboard."

## E5 — Classification
Categories, types, or groupings of things.
**Signals:** "types of", buckets, a small taxonomy. **Example:** "Three categories of risk."

## E6 — Comparison
A vs B, trade-offs, before/after.
**Signals:** "versus", pros/cons, "the difference between". **Example:** "Manual vs automated."

## E7 — Cause & Effect
Relationships, consequences, dependencies.
**Signals:** "because", "leads to", "if… then" (explanatory). **Example:** "Why late replies lose customers."

## E8 — Example / Case
A worked example, scenario, or case study illustrating a concept.
**Signals:** "for example", a named case, a story. **Example:** "How Kedai Ali cut admin by half."

## E9 — Fact / Data
Figures, statistics, or reference data.
**Signals:** numbers, tables, citations. **Example:** "40% of the week goes to admin."

## E10 — Decision / Judgment
A choice the learner makes, with consequences.
**Signals:** "what would you do", roleplay, a fork. **Example:** "An angry customer calls — respond."

## E11 — Assessment / Check
A recall or knowledge check that confirms understanding.
**Signals:** a question with options, "select the correct". **Example:** "Which step comes first?"

## E12 — Calculation / Estimation
Quantitative content where inputs produce a tailored result.
**Signals:** a formula, inputs → output, ROI/what-if. **Example:** "Estimate your yearly savings."

## E13 — Warning / Critical
Must-not-miss safety, compliance, or legal information.
**Signals:** "danger/warning", "mandatory", regulatory text. **Example:** "Never share the master key."
**Always visible — never hidden behind an interaction or gate.**

## E14 — Motivation / Framing
A hook, why-it-matters, opening, or transition that sets context/tone.
**Signals:** a title, a quote, an emotional opener. **Example:** "Run the business, not the busywork."

## E15 — Summary / Recap
Consolidation of prior content; key takeaways.
**Signals:** "in summary", recap, "key points". **Example:** "Three things to remember."

---

### Optional cognitive level (`bloom`)
Any element may be annotated with a Bloom level for governance/reporting:
`remember · understand · apply · analyze · evaluate · create`. Optional, not required.

### Frequency note
`E1`–`E5`, `E11`, `E12`, `E14`, `E15` are common in most courseware. `E6`, `E7`, `E8`, `E10`, `E13`
are rarer but high-value where they apply. (Kept in the set for coverage; trim later based on usage.)
